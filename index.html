By Order of the Onion

The State of the Onion is a solemn and highly unserious gathering devoted to the study, celebration, and competitive enjoyment of onions. Guests should expect expert presentations, ceremonial proceedings, onion-forward athletic contests, a dip competition, BBQ, awards, and the formal State of the Onion Address.

Attendees are encouraged to arrive with curiosity, good humor, and strong opinions about onion varieties.

🏛️

Official Proceedings

Opening ceremonies, presentation of the blooming onion, and formal recognition of onion-related excellence.

🥣

Dip Competition

Bring a dip to enter the exhibition. Awards may include fan favorite, most creative, and best in show.

🎓

Expert Presentations

Distinguished speakers will address topics of urgent allium importance.

Official Program of Events

Event Schedule

3:10 p.m.

Presentation of the Blooming Onion and gaveling into session

Outside on the deck

The presentation of the Blooming Onion, courtesy of Outback Steakhouse, marks the kickoff of State of the Onion and will be immediately followed by a summary of the afternoon’s events.

3:00–6:00 p.m.

Dip competition

Dining room

Visit the dip competition to taste a host of dips containing onions and vote for the best one. Awards will be given for best in show, most creative, and fan favorite.

3:00–6:30 p.m.

BBQ

Outside on the deck

Throughout the event, enjoy hot dogs, hamburgers, cheeseburgers, veggie burgers, pasta salad, caesar salad, and assorted snacks on the deck. Take a break to network with other attendees, reflect on your learnings, and energize for upcoming sessions!

3:15 p.m.

Demonstration: How to cut an onion (Bree Babcock)

Dining room

You use them every day, so how many hours of your life have been wasted on poor form cutting onions? Experts will demonstrate their techniques for efficient, precise onion cutting. If time allows, audience members (13+) may have the opportunity to try cutting an onion.

3:45 p.m.

Onion sack race (pre-registration required)

Backyard

Attendees will compete in a physical challenge rivaling the potato sack races of yore. Non-participants are invited to spectate from the deck. First place winner will be awarded a prize.

4:00 p.m.

Presentation: Different kinds of onions and what they’re for (James Brosch)

Living room

Vidalia? Yellow? Spring? You’ve seen them in the store, but how are onions different and which should you use for your next recipe? Experts will present on common onion varieties and their purposes in the kitchen, then take questions from the audience.

4:30 p.m.

Onion ring toss

Backyard

All attendees are invited to participate in an onion ring tossing event, with the most successful ring earning a prize.

4:50 p.m.

Presentation: Peculiarities of the onion economy (Adam Bee)

Living room

You may have lived with them for years without knowing that onions are different from all other commodities in the United States - ready to find out how? Join experts for a presentation on peculiarities of the onion economy, with plenty of time for questions.

5:20 p.m.

Presentation: What onions teach us about innovation (Brianna Cowell)

Living room

Any product as common as the onion is an opportunity for invention - a chance to analyze pain points and identify monetizable solutions. We'll inspect onion-adjacent inventions and how they demonstrate the power and potential of innovation.

5:50 p.m.

Presentation of prizes

Backyard

6:00 p.m.

State of the Onion Address

Backyard
